Our Comprehensive Squirrel Removal Process

Our squirrel removal process is thorough and designed to not only eliminate the current infestation but also prevent future issues. We follow a systematic approach to ensure effective and lasting results. The first step involves a detailed inspection to understand the extent of the problem and identify entry points.

Initial Inspection and Assessment

A skilled technician will conduct a comprehensive inspection of your property. This includes examining your attic, crawl spaces, roofline, and any other areas where squirrels are likely to nest or enter. We look for signs of squirrel activity, such as droppings, nesting materials, chewed wood, and insulation damage.

Identifying Entry Points

Understanding how squirrels are getting into your home is paramount. Squirrels are adept climbers and can squeeze through surprisingly small openings. We meticulously identify all potential entry points, including gaps in siding, damaged vents, uncapped chimneys, and holes around pipes or wires. This step is crucial for effective prevention.

Humane Trapping and Removal

Once the entry points are identified and the extent of the infestation is assessed, we implement humane trapping methods. We strategically place traps in areas where squirrels are active. Our goal is to capture the squirrels safely without causing harm.

Safe Relocation Procedures

After squirrels are trapped, we adhere to strict guidelines for their relocation. We transport the trapped squirrels to an appropriate location far from your property, in accordance with local regulations and best practices for wildlife management. This ensures they don't simply return to your home.

Exclusion and Prevention

Removing the squirrels is only half the battle. To prevent future infestations, we offer exclusion services. This involves sealing up all identified entry points using durable, squirrel-resistant materials. This is a critical step in ensuring a long-term solution to your squirrel problem.

Repairing Damage

Squirrels can cause significant damage to your property. We assess and can recommend repairs for areas affected by squirrel activity, such as damaged insulation, chewed wiring, and structural damage. Addressing these issues is essential for the safety and integrity of your home.

Signs You May Need Squirrel Removal Services

Recognizing the signs of a squirrel infestation early is key to preventing extensive damage and health risks. Paying attention to subtle indicators can help you address the problem before it escalates. Squirrels are often most active during the day, especially in the morning and late afternoon.

Noises in the Attic or Walls

One of the most common signs of a squirrel infestation is hearing scratching, scurrying, or gnawing sounds coming from your attic, walls, or even chimneys. These noises are typically most noticeable during daylight hours as the squirrels move around their nest sites. If you hear these sounds, it's a strong indicator that you may have unwelcome guests.

These sounds can be particularly disturbing, especially at night when your home is quiet. The constant movement and chewing can disrupt your sleep and create an unsettling atmosphere. Ignoring these auditory signals can lead to more significant issues as the squirrel population grows and the damage to your property increases. Identifying the location of these noises can help our technicians pinpoint areas of activity during the inspection phase.

Visible Damage to Your Property

Squirrels have a natural instinct to chew. They may chew on wood, wires, insulation, and even plastic pipes. This can lead to visible damage on the exterior and interior of your home. Look for chewed openings along the roofline, damaged vents, and signs of gnawing around windows or doors. Inside, you might find scattered insulation or frayed wires.

The damage caused by squirrels is not merely cosmetic. Chewed electrical wires pose a serious fire hazard, and damaged insulation can lead to increased energy costs and reduced comfort in your home. Structural damage to wood supports or siding can compromise the integrity of your building. Addressing this damage is crucial for the safety and longevity of your property, and our service includes evaluating and recommending necessary repairs after removing the pests.

Droppings and Urine Stains

Finding squirrel droppings or urine stains in areas like your attic, garage, or shed is a clear indication of an infestation. Squirrel droppings are small, pellet-like, and often scattered irregularly. Urine can create strong odors and may stain insulation or wood surfaces. These waste products not only create unsanitary conditions but can also attract other pests.

Squirrel waste can carry pathogens that are harmful to human health. Breathing in dust contaminated with droppings can potentially lead to respiratory issues. The presence of droppings and urine indicates that squirrels have established a nesting site within your property, making professional removal necessary. Cleaning up these contaminated areas after removal is an important step in restoring a healthy indoor environment, and we can provide guidance on proper sanitation procedures.

Sightings of Squirrels Entering or Exiting

If you frequently see squirrels entering or exiting your home through specific openings, it's undeniable proof of an infestation. Observe where they are coming and going from to help identify their access points. Common entry points include gaps under eaves, damaged chimneys, and broken vents.

Observing squirrel behavior can provide valuable clues as to how they are accessing your property and where they might be nesting. Noticing squirrels consistently using the same route to enter your home confirms that they have found a reliable way in. This information assists our technicians in strategically placing traps and planning the exclusion process. It's important not to attempt to block these entry points yourself without ensuring all squirrels are out, as this could trap them inside.

Nesting Material Accumulation

Squirrels build nests using a variety of materials, including leaves, twigs, insulation, and shredded fabric. Finding accumulations of these materials in areas like the attic, walls, or chimney is a strong indication of a squirrel nest. These nests are where they shelter and raise their young.

Squirrel nests in your home create a fire risk, especially if they are built near electrical wiring. Furthermore, nesting materials can clog vents and create blockages, affecting ventilation. The presence of a nest often means that there are also young squirrels present, which requires a slightly different approach to removal to ensure the safety of both the mother and her offspring. Our techniques are designed to humanely handle situations involving nesting squirrels and their young.

Unpleasant Odors

A persistent, foul odor in certain areas of your home, particularly in the attic or walls, can be a result of squirrel urine, droppings, or even deceased animals. These odors can be strong and unpleasant, significantly impacting the air quality within your home.

The smell associated with squirrel waste can be quite powerful and can permeate through walls and ceilings, affecting your comfort and potentially indicating unsanitary conditions. A dead squirrel within a wall void or attic space can also produce a very strong and offensive odor as it decomposes. Professional removal addresses not only the living squirrels but also the cleanup and remediation of contaminated areas to eliminate these unpleasant smells and associated health risks. Explore our rodent control services for other types of unwanted visitors.

Increased Number of Squirrels on Your Property

While seeing a squirrel occasionally in your yard is normal, an unusual increase in the number of squirrels on your property, especially around your home, could indicate that they have found a comfortable place to live and reproduce nearby. They may be attracted by easy access to food sources or potential den sites.

A noticeable surge in the squirrel population around your home can be a precursor to an indoor infestation. As squirrels reproduce, the competition for resources and nesting sites increases, driving them to seek shelter in places that offer protection and warmth, such as your home's attic or walls. Identifying and addressing these outdoor attractants, in addition to indoor removal, is part of our comprehensive approach to squirrel management. This can include securing trash cans and eliminating easily accessible food sources.

Damaged Gutters or Fascia

Squirrels are agile and can exploit weaknesses in your home's structure to gain entry. Damaged gutters or fascia boards provide easy access points for squirrels to reach the roofline and find ways into your attic or other areas of your home. Chewing on these materials is also common.

Maintaining the exterior of your home is important for preventing animal intrusions. Damaged gutters can hold standing water, attracting pests, and damaged fascia provides a direct entry point into your roof structure. Squirrels can chew through weakened wood or enlarge existing openings to get inside. Our inspection process includes examining these areas to identify potential or existing entry points and can recommend repairs to secure your home against future invasions. Consider our residential pest control services for a holistic approach.

Sounds of Chewing or Gnawing

Beyond the scratching and scurrying, you might distinctly hear sounds of chewing or gnawing. Squirrels need to constantly chew to keep their teeth trimmed. They will chew on wood, wires, pipes, and anything else available, which is why they cause so much damage inside homes.

The sound of persistent chewing from within your walls or ceiling is a clear indicator that squirrels are actively damaging your property. This is not just an annoyance; it's a sign that vital structural or electrical components of your home are being compromised. Pinpointing the location of these chewing sounds helps our technicians target their efforts and assess the extent of the damage that needs addressing. Hearing these sounds should prompt immediate action to prevent further destruction and potential hazards.

Electrical Issues

Chewed electrical wires caused by squirrels can lead to serious electrical issues, including power outages, flickering lights, or even a burning smell. These issues are incredibly dangerous and significantly increase the risk of a house fire. Any unexplained electrical problems could be a sign of rodent or squirrel damage.

The danger posed by squirrels chewing on electrical wiring cannot be overstated. Exposed wires create a direct fire hazard that can have devastating consequences. If you experience unexplained electrical malfunctions and suspect squirrels, it is crucial to address the issue with urgency. Our service focuses on removing the squirrels and can help identify areas of damaged wiring that require professional repair by a qualified electrician to ensure the safety of your home. This is a key reason why timely squirrel removal is so important.

Insulation Damage

Squirrels often use insulation as nesting material, pulling it apart and compacting it. This damages the insulation, reducing its effectiveness in regulating your home's temperature. You might notice areas where insulation is disturbed, matted down, or missing.

Damaged insulation not only compromises your home's energy efficiency, leading to higher heating and cooling bills, but it also creates an unsanitary environment due to squirrel waste. Replacing contaminated insulation is necessary to restore the health and comfort of your indoor space. Our process includes evaluating the condition of your insulation and can provide recommendations for remediation or replacement after the squirrels have been removed and the entry points sealed to prevent future issues. For other pests that might cause insulation damage, consider our bed bug extermination or cockroach control services.

Common Squirrel Problems We Solve

Squirrels, while seemingly harmless from a distance, can cause a range of significant problems when they decide to make your home their own. Our squirrel removal services in Gardena, CA, are specifically designed to address and resolve these common issues, protecting your property and ensuring your safety. Understanding the types of problems squirrels create highlights the importance of professional intervention when an infestation occurs. They are opportunistic animals and will take advantage of any weaknesses they find in your home's structure.

One of the most frequent and concerning problems is moth control, often overlooked until damage is visible. Squirrels, much like moths, can cause significant damage in hidden areas. When squirrels gain access to attics, they often chew on electrical wiring. This is an instinctual behavior, driven by their need to wear down always-growing teeth. However, it leads to exposed wires and a severe fire hazard, putting your entire home at risk. Our technicians are trained to identify areas of chewed wiring during the inspection and can advise on necessary repairs to mitigate this danger. This not only increases your heating and cooling costs but also creates an unhygienic environment polluted with squirrel droppings and urine. The presence of these waste materials can lead to unpleasant odors and potential health risks. We address this by removing the squirrels and providing options for cleaning or replacing damaged and contaminated insulation to restore the air quality and thermal efficiency of your home. Effective squirrel removal also helps prevent secondary pest issues, such as those requiring mosquito control if standing water collects due to damage or tick control if wildlife brings them in.

Structural damage is also a significant concern. Squirrels can chew through wood siding, fascia boards, eaves, and even roofing materials to enter your home. Our exclusion services focus on repairing these damaged areas with durable materials that squirrels cannot easily chew through, effectively sealing your home against future intrusions. Unpleasant odors resulting from squirrel urine and droppings are a common complaint.
